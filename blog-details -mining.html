MINING ENGINEERING Dept/Details

The Department of Mining Engineering focuses on the study and application of engineering principles to extract minerals from the earth safely and efficiently. It covers topics such as mineral processing, mine design, safety, environmental impact, and sustainable resource management. The department prepares students for careers in mining operations, research, and industry leadership, combining theoretical knowledge with practical, hands-on experience in mining technology.

DEPARTMENT OF MINING ENGINEERING

  • Dr. Manas Mukhopadhyay

    • Academic Programs:
    The department typically offers undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral programs in mining engineering. These programs are designed to provide a solid foundation in engineering principles while focusing on the specific skills needed for mining operations. The curriculum typically covers subjects such as geology, mineral exploration, rock mechanics, mine design, drilling and blasting techniques, environmental impact assessment, and mineral processing.

    For graduate students, the department often provides advanced studies in specialized areas like mine safety, automation and robotics in mining, energy resources, and sustainable mining practices. The doctoral programs usually emphasize research and development in cutting-edge technologies, with students contributing to innovations that improve mining efficiency, safety, and environmental sustainability.

    Research and Innovation:
    The Department of Mining Engineering is often involved in groundbreaking research that tackles critical issues facing the mining industry today. These include developing new technologies for mineral extraction, improving energy efficiency, and reducing the environmental footprint of mining operations. Research projects may also focus on advanced mine planning, rock mechanics, automated mining systems, and the management of mining waste. Faculty members are often engaged in collaborative research with industry partners, government agencies, and other academic institutions. These collaborations ensure that students gain exposure to the latest developments in the field and provide opportunities for hands-on research experience.

    Industry Collaboration and Internships:
    One of the key strengths of a mining engineering department is its close ties with the mining industry. Many programs offer internship opportunities with mining companies, which allow students to gain practical experience in real-world mining operations. These internships are crucial for developing a deep understanding of the challenges and nuances of the industry.

    The department may also host industry speakers, seminars, and workshops that provide students with insight into the latest trends, technologies, and career opportunities in mining. Additionally, many departments have established partnerships with mining companies to create research and training opportunities, further bridging the gap between academic learning and industry practice.

    DEPARTMENT OF MINING ENGINEERING

    The Department of Mining Engineering offers comprehensive programs focused on mining operations, safety, and sustainability. Key features include hands-on experience through internships and fieldwork, cutting-edge research in mining technologies, and strong industry collaboration. The curriculum covers topics like mine design, mineral processing, rock mechanics, and environmental management. The department emphasizes safety, sustainable practices, and innovation, preparing students to lead in the global mining industry. Students also engage in practical learning through research projects and partnerships with mining companies.

Dr. Manas Mukhopadhyay

Dr. Manas Mukhopadhyay is the Professor & Head of the Department of Mining Engineering at DRTTIT. With a Ph.D. in Slope Stability, he has 28 years of teaching experience, 2.5 years of industrial experience, and 3 years of research experience. He has published 10 technical papers in national/international journals. Dr. Mukhopadhyay guides 70 UG students and holds leadership roles in R&D, Consultancy, and ERC. He is also a member of MEAI and a BOS member at VTU. Email: dr.manas@drttit.edu.in.
